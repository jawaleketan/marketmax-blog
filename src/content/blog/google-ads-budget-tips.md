Think you need a huge budget to succeed with Google Ads? Think again. Small budgets just force you to be smarter.

## 1. Start with Exact Match Keywords

Broad match keywords can burn through your budget fast. Start with exact match to control exactly what queries trigger your ads.

## 2. Use Negative Keywords

Negative keywords prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches — the fastest way to reduce wasted spend. Common negatives: "free," "jobs," "salary," "courses."

## 3. Optimize for Quality Score

Google rewards relevant ads with lower costs. Higher Quality Score = lower cost per click. Focus on expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page quality.

## 4. Focus on Long-Tail Keywords

Long-tail keywords have lower competition and higher conversion intent.

## 5. Schedule Your Ads

If your business doesn't get calls at 2 AM, don't show ads at 2 AM.

## 6. Target by Location

Don't waste budget showing ads nationwide if you only serve specific cities or regions.

## 7. Test, Measure, Repeat

Every week: pause underperformers, increase bids on top performers, test new ad copy, review search term reports.

## The Bottom Line

A small PPC budget isn't a disadvantage. It forces discipline. Focus on exact match keywords, negative keywords, Quality Score, and continuous optimization.
